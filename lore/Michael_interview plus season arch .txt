0) QUICK EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
• Michael, a lower-class angel/artist with envy and hope in equal measure, stands at the threshold of a mythic corporate machine (Heaven Inc.).
• Episode 1 delivers his first temptation and false ascent (mall → arcade → token → Juice → viral attention → job offer).
• Episode 2 transitions into the fluorescent satire of office heaven: interview, shallow validation, illusions of choice, anxiety crash, manipulative mentorship, “aha” clarity, and a golden but isolating outcome.
• Tarot spreads were used as development x-rays:
  – 5-card “Crossroads of Becoming” defines the pilot’s inner weather.
  – 7-card “Ladder of Awakening” cleanly maps Episode 2’s emotional/structural rhythm.
• Season 1 ends in fame → investigation → cancellation → exile from Heaven; the middle now has a mapped tonal corridor (‘The Apartment’ meets ‘Brazil’).

1) PROJECT SPINE & NORTH STAR
Essence: Spiritual noir about art vs. propaganda inside a corporate afterlife.
Cosmology: Universe runs on divine code; God is the CEO of light; the Devil is the revolutionary who introduced autonomy (chaos) into creation; Dave Juice is liquid access to awareness and code.
Season 1 Thesis: “A tired angel sells his soul just to feel alive for a night.” He rises via illicit inspiration, becomes a brand, and is expelled when truth and desire break the PR spell.
North Star Line: God wrote the rules. The Devil taught them to dance.

2) PROTAGONIST: INTERVIEW-DERIVED PSYCHOLOGY (PRE–EPISODE 1)
(Collected from the character interview exercise and user answers.)

BEST DAY
• “Heaven Disneyland” childhood trip — wonder, spectacle, innocence. He equates holiness with curated magic and communal awe.

WORST DAY
• Discovery that Gabriel and Liza stole/claimed their collaborative art. That was the death of naïveté. From that day: fear of betrayal, distrust, envy as a scar.

PRIDE
• “Making art, helping people.” Creation = prayer. Empathy = integrity.

FEARS
• Death-by-stagnation. Returning home a failure to hear “I told you so.” Not making it as an artist. Being ordinary. Becoming the hollow suit.

DESIRE
• “Lots of money.” Translation: safety, independence, insulation from humiliation; proof he matters.

HIDDEN TRUTHS
• “I’m God’s number one fan. I’m very unlucky.” Parasocial faith + learned helplessness. Believes success is divine attention; failure = being unseen by God.

BELIEFS ABOUT WORLD
• “The world has a chance to be good; there is beauty.” Core romantic; the light isn’t totally a lie.

MIRROR/SHAME
• Sees “someone not living up to their potential.” Fears being a loser. Inner critic is vicious; halo of shame.

LOST HAPPINESS
• Childhood + the trio (Liza, Gabriel): making art, movies, Disneyland warmth. Nostalgia is sacred fuel and trap.

AVOIDANCE
• “Taking my life into my own hands more seriously.” Works hard but avoids decisive authorship; waits for permission/destiny rather than chooses.

ONE-LINE PSYCH PORTRAIT
• A romantic cynic: worships light, fears it, and longs to deserve it.

3) TAROT DIAGNOSTICS — EPISODE 1
Spread: “Crossroads of Becoming” (5 cards, in order)
Cards: Seven of Swords → Seven of Pentacles → Queen of Swords → Page of Cups → Wheel of Fortune

POSITION & ESSENCE
1) The Mask / Present Conflict — Seven of Swords
   • Self-protective cunning, hidden longing, alienation; inner trespass precedes outer act.
2) The Grind / Exhaustion — Seven of Pentacles
   • Limbo fatigue; effort without harvest; spiritual stagnation.
3) The Armor / Persona — Queen of Swords
   • Cool intellect as shield; irony over intimacy; pain edited into wit.
4) The Seed / Buried Soul — Page of Cups
   • Tender artist-child; wonder trying to reemerge; emotional risk returns.
5) The Turning / System Moves — Wheel of Fortune
   • External pivot; inevitability; the machine answers his secret wish.

Pilot Implication (non-plot): From guarded stagnation to accidental awakening.

Cards: Two of Swords → Six of Wands → Seven of Cups → Nine of Swords → King of Cups → Ace of Swords → Nine of Pentacles

POSITION & ESSENCE

   • Indecision, suspended decision, fear of consequence.

   • Recognition, applause, confidence bump (ego risk).
3) Mirage — Seven of Cups
   • Illusory options, dazzled by dreams; need discernment.
4) Nightmare — Nine of Swords
   • Anxiety crash; self-punishment; sleepless doubt.
5) Mentor/Mirror — King of Cups
   • Emotional mastery, soothing authority; could heal or subtly manipulate.
6) Realization — Ace of Swords
   • Cognitive breakthrough; truth blade; clean mental line.
7) Reward/Cost — Nine of Pentacles
   • Material success, independence, serene isolation; golden garden with walls.

Episode 2 Implication (non-plot): A complete emotional rise that lands in poised solitude.

5) EPISODE 1 — SCENE THESIS & ARCADE CONCEPT
Emotional Thesis: The mall is a benevolent trap: a memory palace where nostalgia is monetized.
Arcade of Light: A late-night 80s arcade, CRT glow, synth-pop loop. Clerk hums the trumpet motif. A game of chance yields an innocent prize (toy/halo/keychain) that later “keys” a system. The win feels like luck; it is destiny’s soft shove.
Function: Invisible threshold from ennui to enchantment; the moment the universe starts answering him.

7) SEASON 1 STRUCTURE (10-EP SKETCH) & TONAL PHASES
Title thread: “The Price of Light”

EP1 The Cost of Light — Pilot temptation and first ascent; morning after = job offer.
EP2 The Interview — Fluorescent satire; validation-illusion-clarity; hired.
EP3 Welcome Package — Orientation absurdism; rules of divine bureaucracy; allies emerge.
EP4 The Brainstorm — First creative win; status trinket; second sip temptation.
EP5 Office Party of Angels — Gala seduction; Devil’s charming mask; love triangle twists.
EP6 The Campaign — Heaven 2.0 PR; God’s televised praise; creative mania; Eden prototype.
EP7 Thought Police — Surveillance pressure; paranoia; confiscation; mentor’s mask tilts.
EP8 Golden Cage — Wealth, penthouse, loneliness; offer from Devil; line crossed.
EP9 The Fall — Public crash; cancellation; pursuit; flight through lower levels.
EP10 Exile — Alone below Heaven; unresolved trumpet motif finally lands on a human note.

Tonal Phases
• Early (1–2): Wistful realism (curiosity, hope)
• Middle (3–6): Satirical absurdism (temptation, humor, spectacle)
• Late (7–10): Tragic noir × mystical (paranoia, consequence, melancholy grace)

8) CHARACTER MATRIX
MICHAEL — 24, lower-class angel, artist’s soul. Flaws: envy, passivity, self-doubt. Virtues: empathy, creativity, hope. Desire: money/safety; secret desire: to be chosen.
GOD — CEO-performer; on-air charisma, off-air control; aesthetics as power; “Heaven 2.0” architect.
DEVIL (Samael) — Revolutionary hacker; comic-tragic mentor; speaks through glitches, mirrors, music; sells freedom with a wink.
LIZA — The Muse turned professional; warmth with boundaries; caught between loyalty and truth.
GABRIEL — Ambitious executive creative; opportunist; the mirror of who Michael might become.
SERAPH / THOUGHT POLICE — Elegant menace; runs debt and enforcement; aestheticized cruelty.

Key Dynamics
• Michael ↔ God: adoration souring into disillusion.
• Michael ↔ Devil: mentorship-as-temptation; humor as honesty.
• Michael ↔ Liza: embers of love vs. career gravity.
• Michael ↔ Gabriel: sibling rivalry; stolen authorship; status theater.

14) OPEN QUESTIONS, RISKS, WRITER PROMPTS
OPEN QUESTIONS
• Exact nature of Michael’s first public miracle? (Keep small, personal, evidentiary.)
• Liza’s agency: what secret line will she not cross? When does she choose truth over career?
• Devil’s boundary: does he ever lie, or only omit?
• What, precisely, triggers Thought Police suspicion? (Telemetry? Art anomalies?)
• How literal is code-editing in S1? (Keep “view-only” early; crafting over god-mode.)

RISKS
• Over-saturation of mythology in early eps — protect human scale.
• Glamour without consequence — ensure every win seeds a cost.
• Devil as pure mentor — maintain ambiguity and price.

WRITER PROMPTS
• Write a 1-page scene: Devil-as-janitor teaching Michael how to breathe before a pitch (“jazz breath”).
• Compose a 10-line “God Hour” monologue that both flatters workers and threatens dissent.
• Draft the scent/sound list of the arcade from Michael’s POV; no visuals, only senses.

15) PRODUCTION NOTES & ITERATION GUIDELINES
• Keep Episode 1 intimate and visual; minimize exposition. Let tarot emotions drive blocking and light.
• For Episode 2, use the 7-rung ladder as your beat sheet; each rung = 1–2 scenes.
• Treat music as narrative glue (trumpet → Devil; choir sting → God; synth wash → code).
• Evidence Rule: art is always evidence. Success exposes him further.
• Maintain options; do not lock canon that inhibits later discovery.
