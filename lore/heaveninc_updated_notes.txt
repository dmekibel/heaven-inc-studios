
# Heaven Inc. – Updated Conception Notes (2025)

These updates reflect significant new directions, refinements, and missing elements compared to the original project files. They should be integrated to bring the Heaven Inc. story world fully up to date.

---

## 1. Protagonist Setup (Michael)
- Unlike earlier drafts where Michael begins in suburbia, the new conception often places him as a **busboy in Celestial Manhattan** (a luxury jazz bar where execs from Heaven Inc. drink and socialize).
- The tone is slower, character-driven, showing Michael’s daily life, frustrations, and longing before any supernatural event (inspired by Stanley Ipkiss in *The Mask*).
- Mise-en-scène: Michael’s **shitty cubicle apartment**, broken fridge, cracked mirrors, him walking through the city streets, **staring enviously at execs in glowing suits** and expensive storefronts he can’t afford.
- His character is defined by **envy, yearning, and creativity** rather than immediate plot propulsion.

---

## 2. The Vending Machine as Mythic Object
- Old notes feature Michael stealing a keycard or breaking into Heaven Inc. through a recruiter or theft【16†heaveninc.txt†L7-L15】.
- New version: the **exec-only vending machine** is the mythic threshold object, hidden in restricted areas of the Heaven Lounge or Dream Studio.
- It is a **luxury, retro-futurist altar-machine** only accessible by chance, favor, or cleverness. Access is earned through luck, glitches, or helping someone—not handed out directly.
- Michael obsesses over this machine through **rumors** before he ever sees it.

---

## 3. The Special Drink
- Earlier concept: Dave Juice is already central, with the Devil obsessed and later drinking an entire can【7†heaveninc.txt†L23-L23】.
- Updated: Michael encounters **an “off-menu” special flavor** (Lucifer Grape, Prototype, etc.) that appears as a glitch option when he tries the vending machine.
- He receives the can through a mix of **luck and fate**, reinforcing folklore vibes (like Persephone’s pomegranate or Coraline’s little door).

---

## 4. Pacing of the Discovery
- Instead of drinking immediately, Michael is **forced to hide the can** when execs arrive.  
- He takes it home and almost forgets it (possibly thinking it was a drunken dream).  
- This slow-burn pacing adds **mystery and inevitability**, unlike the faster escalations in older notes.

---

## 5. The First Sip and Memory Flashback
- When he finally drinks, it does **not reveal the Devil directly**.  
- Instead, the drink triggers a **flashback**: his past betrayal, when his best friend stole his work and got hired at Heaven Inc., and Shasta followed the friend【16†heaveninc.txt†L7-L19】.  
- This deepens the curse/memory/mythic dimension of the drink—it dredges up destiny and injustice rather than just giving him powers.

---

## 6. Subtle Devil Influence
- The Devil is **not revealed directly** in the pilot. His presence is only implied through vending machine glitches, whispers, or faint reflections.  
- This is slower, more folkloric, and more mythic than older drafts, which featured more direct Devil mentorship early【13†heaveninc.txt†L1-L9】.

---

## 7. Consequences of the Sip
- After the sip, Michael unexpectedly gains recognition:  
  - One of his ideas “submits itself” and trends in Heaven Inc.’s internal cloud.  
  - He is offered a **junior creative job**, starting his ascent.  
- This mirrors folklore “deal with the devil” bargains but presented through **corporate bureaucracy**.

---

## 8. Tone and Inspirations
- Stronger emphasis now on:  
  - **Slow pacing and mise-en-scène** (walking through city, watching execs, looking at suits).  
  - **Fairy tale / myth parallels** (Coraline’s door, Persephone’s fruit, LOTR’s One Ring).  
  - **Greek tragedy layering** (envy, betrayal, fate, cursed gifts).  
  - **Biblical echoes** (the forbidden fruit, false prophets, Cain and Abel dynamic).  

---

## Summary of What’s Missing in Original Files
1. Michael as **busboy in Manhattan** (instead of just suburban angel).  
2. The **exec-only vending machine** as the mythic object of desire.  
3. The **special off-menu Dave Juice flavor** offered by glitch.  
4. **Slow-burn pacing**: scenes of him at home, in the city, envying suits, building character before the supernatural.  
5. **He hides the can, forgets it, and rediscovers it later**—rather than instantly using it.  
6. The **first sip triggers flashbacks to betrayal**, not just empowerment.  
7. The Devil remains **hidden, implied, folkloric**—not a direct mentor yet.  
8. Aesthetic emphasis on **folklore, myth, LOTR, Coraline, Biblical stories** more than just satire.
